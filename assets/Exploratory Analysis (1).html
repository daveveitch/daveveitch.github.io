Note that the default does not happen immediately, but there is a pattern where someone is delinquent for awhile, and then it is sold which is represented by 'R'. Also note that once something defaults it is not until a few months later that the actual loss shows up.

Also it is worth noting the calculation of Actual Loss:

Actual Loss = (Default UPB Net Sale_Proceeds) + Delinquent Accrued Interest - Expenses MI Recoveries Non MI Recoveries

Delinquent Accrued Interest = (Default_UPB Non Interest bearing UPB) (Current Interest rate 0.35) ( Months between Last Principal & Interest paid to date and zero balance date ) * 30/360/100

0.35 is the assumed servicing fee

So even though the sale price will be higher than the default UPB you can still have positive actual losses.
